Bleeding from the mucosa of the stomach after abdominal operations - apparently also due to toxic causes and not to the operation - gives rise to the so-called post-operative haematemesis. 
Constitutional Effects of hemorrhage # # -. The severity of symptoms resulting from the bleeding depends on the speed with which the bleeding occurs and the amount of blood lost. 

The sudden loss of a lot, either from an open wound or in a serous cavity - for example, after rupture of the liver or spleen - is accompanied by marked pallor of the body surface and the coldness of the skin, especially the face, feet and hands. The skin is wet, cold, clammy sweat, and beads of sweat stand out on his forehead. 

The pulse becomes weak, soft, fast, and the patient is dull and listless, and complains of extreme thirst. The temperature is usually lower than normal; and rapid, shallow breathing, and sighing in character. Abnormal visual sensations in the form of flashing lights or spots in the eyes, and by land, buzzing, roaring or ringing in the ears, have been reported. 

In extreme cases, phenomena that have been aptly described as that of "air hunger" ensue. Due to the small amount of blood that circulates throughout the body, and decreased hemoglobin content of blood, tissues are imperfectly oxygenated, and the patient becomes extremely restless, gasping for breath, constantly throwing about his arms and exposing her chest in a vain attempt to breathe more freely.
