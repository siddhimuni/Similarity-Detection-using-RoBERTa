Her aunt is kind, indulgent,and pitying her. In youth she had experienced of a blighted affection, and no women-heart, that is not naturally sour, passes through such trial without becoming sweeter. She was cound in complete serfdom to her brother, and was quite unable to suggest any means or likelihood of release. Barbara wrote a full account of her predicament to her lover. She disguised by dress to decieve even herself, she was again at Aberdeen. Should all the other methods fail, to carry off his kinswomen on the very eve of the bridal. When the minister sat over his books or took his dinner nap, did those two young creatures meet, unnoticed and unsuspected. But those meetings must soon end, for six months have passed, and Mr. Bruce--once more lodged in the house of Davy Bain--is come to wed and take home his reluctant bride. 

One evening--it was cloudy and threatened foul weather, though the summer air was warm--John Percival betook himself as usual to the customary trysting-place. It was a thick copse of hazel past which ran--heard but not seen--the river; which, where the shrubbery ended, formed a dark, deep pool, so garnished by overhanging nut-trees that it had acquired the name of the Nut-hole.Beyond this pool lay the road to the manse, the Nut-tree-hole became the limits to Percival's attendance on his cousin in her way homeward.
