According to He dyed Dom. 1630, the Christian name of Parsons was not William, but Walter. The Christian name of Parsons' successor, Evan, was William. 

The bas-relief noted in the writings reads "The King's Porter, and the Dwarf" and is dated 1660. It shows William Evans and his servant, Jeffrey Hudson and resides above the Bull-head Court, Newgate Street entrance. 

At Hampton Court, there is a picture of Queen Elizabeth's giant porter, but no portait of Parsons. 

Lord Braybrooke suggests, Pepys' friends drank two quarts of wormwood wine given to them by Pepys himself. However, this is a bitter, unpalatable drink, and must have taken a large group to drink that much.
