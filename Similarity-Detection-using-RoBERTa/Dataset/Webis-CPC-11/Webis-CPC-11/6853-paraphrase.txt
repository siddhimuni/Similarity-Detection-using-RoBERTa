The horses were very tired and hungry having survived on very little the past two day, the decided to rest for a few days, while the looked closley at the river towards its mouth.

I started with Mr. Bedart, and tracing the stream downwards to the south-west, we would reach the sea after a ride of six hours. thier would be narrow strips of land on each side and the country would be very differdnt. the hills consist of sandstone and sand coverd with scrub and a gigantic plains of grass, the leaves were no feed for the horses but instead it drove the horses crazy. The leaves were called boyonet grass for they were armed with sharp thorny points
