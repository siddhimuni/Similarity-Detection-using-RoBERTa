King William provided great material, either poetry or prose. He was really in his mind before what he is in his lyrics, he considers a hero, and was accustomed to say that he praised others in compliance with fashion, but that, in celebration of King William followed his inclination. Prior gratitude would dictate praise, which reason would not refuse.

Among the advantages of appearing in future years of the reign of William, he mentions a useful Arts Society, and among them: -

"Some people who really care are taught rhetoric and speech just set our dubious expressions, that our writers from distant realms because we know our monarchs owe, and schools profess our tongue, through every land on which it relies help his hand and blessed him. "

Tickell, in his "prospect of peace", has the same hope of a new academy -

"In happy chains our daring language bound, no longer sport, with an arbitrary sound."

Whether these passages likeness, which has the same thought at the same time, started from accident or imitation, is not easy to determine. Tickell was impressed by his confidence Swift's "Proposal for checking the English language, then lately published.

In Parliament, which met in 1701 he was elected representative of East Grinstead. Maybe it was about this time that he changed his party because he voted for impeachment of the man who persuaded the king to the Partition Treaty, a treaty that he himself was engaged ministerially.
