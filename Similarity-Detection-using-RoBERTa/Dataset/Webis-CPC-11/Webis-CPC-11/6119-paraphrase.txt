Today women in England can not vote on major policy issues of universal state and can not occupy positions of great state. However, their gains have been enormous, as demonstrated below, and in this sense, I also looked briefly to great advances in the colonies. 

In 1850, Ontario led the school of all women's suffrage. In 1867, New South Wales gave them municipal suffrage. In 1869 England granted municipal suffrage to single women and widows, Victoria gave it to all women, married or single. In England, in 1870 the Education Act, by which school boards were created, gave women equal rights with men, both in regard to elect and be elected. In 1871 Western Australia gave municipal suffrage in 1878 New Zealand gave school suffrage. In 1880 South Australia gave municipal suffrage. In 1881 widows and single women won the vote in local elections in Scotland and the parliamentary vote on the Isle of Man Municipal suffrage was given by Ontario and Tasmania in 1884 and by New Zealand and New Brunswick in 1886, by Nova Scotia and Manitoba 1887. In 1888, England gave women county suffrage and British Columbia and the Northern Territory and West gave them municipal suffrage. In 1889 county suffrage was given to women in Scotland and municipal suffrage to single women and widows in the province of Quebec. In 1893 New Zealand gave full voting rights. In 1894 the parish and district suffrage was given in England, married and unmarried women, with the power to elect and be elected to the parish and district councils. In 1895 South Australia gave full state suffrage to all women. In 1898 the women of Ireland were given the right to vote for all officers, except members of Parliament. In 1900 Western Australia granted full state suffrage to all. In 1902 full national suffrage suffrage was given to all women in Australia and the Federated entire state of New South Wales. Full suffrage in 1903 gave the state of Tasmania, Queensland in 1905 did the same in 1908, followed by Victoria. In 1907 England made women eligible as mayors, councilmen, and county and city councilors. In London, for example, women can now vote by the District Councils 28 and 31 boards of guardians of the Council of the City of London, but may be elected, as the central body members are unemployed or 23 committees district, and may be co-opted to all other bodies such as local pension committees. Women may be the rulers of the Council, and there is nothing to prevent one of exploitation, including the president.
