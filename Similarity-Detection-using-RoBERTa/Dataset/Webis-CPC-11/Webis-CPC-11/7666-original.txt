
He died in the belief that he should one day publish an unique work on painting and painters:
such was the aim of his existence, and his study must have been even more curious than the
wonderfully crammed, small house at Islington, where William Upcott, the 'Old Mortality' in
his line, who saved from the housemaid's fire-lighting designs the MSS. Of Evelyn's

Life and Letters, which he found tossing about in the old gallery at Wotton, near Dorking,
passed his days.
