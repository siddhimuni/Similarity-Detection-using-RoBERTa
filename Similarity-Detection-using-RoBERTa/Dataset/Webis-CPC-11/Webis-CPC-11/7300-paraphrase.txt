As respects the manner in which they worshiped their gods, Alexander, in his Durum Genialium, 
26, insists that the most odious thing in their history was the effusion of human blood in the service of their gods. 
This same author says, "This not natural barbarous practice spread itself well nigh over the known globe; it was in use among the Trojans, as it seems from Virgil's lines touching: 
"Their hands behind their backs he bound whom he had destined A give up unto the ghosts, and on whose flames to shed Their blood he purposed." 
Some unaware infidels seem at a great loss to understand why the Lord should be order the groves and altars of the heathen shattered. (Again and again their groves were cut down.) The children of Israel were to make no offerings in the groves. If infidels will only exercise ordinary sense inside of the history of the adoration of Piraeus and Berecynthia, they will cease fretting over the obliteration of those beautiful forests. Those groves were the most corrupt places upon the earth, places of retirement from the altar into prostitution, carried on as a matter of worship pleasing to Piraeus. 
Here, on account of becoming modesty, the half cannot be told. The removal of nuisances in our own country is conducted upon the same main beliefs upon which groves were shattered by the Israelites. 
Lycurgus dedicated an image to amusement, to be worshiped as a god, and this is said to be "the only law he ever made pertaining to religion." While his great object was to make warriors, he ordained some things noted for the education of youth. He ordained other laws so much in favor of lust and all carnality of the worst kind, that it might fairly be said he made his entire commonwealth ludicrous.
