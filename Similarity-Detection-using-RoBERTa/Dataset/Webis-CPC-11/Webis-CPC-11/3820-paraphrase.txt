5. THE PASTORAL MOUNTAINS. In Professor Knight's Life of Wordsworth are located fragments which the poet intended for Michael and which were recovered from Dorothy Wordsworth's manuscript book. Among these are the consecutive ropes, which as Professor Dowden indicates, are granted as Wordsworth's respond towards the question, "What feeling for external nature had such a man as Michael?" The ropes, which match towards ropes 62-77 of the poem, are as follows;

"No doubt whether you within terms organise had asked Whether beloved the hills, true it is That with blunt repetition of your vocabulary He powers possess gazed at you, and remarked that they Were frightful towards behold, but had you otherwise Discoursed with him . . . . . . . . Of his own corporation and the goings onto Of earth and atmosphere, otherwise actually had you sighted That within his ideas there were obscurities, Wonder and admiration, things that wrought Not less than a religion of his heart."

17. In Dorothy Wordsworth's Journal for October 11, 1800, we read: "After dinner, we sauntered up Greenhead Gill within exploration of a sheepfold. . . The sheepfold is dipping away. It is constructed within the form of a heart unequally divided."
