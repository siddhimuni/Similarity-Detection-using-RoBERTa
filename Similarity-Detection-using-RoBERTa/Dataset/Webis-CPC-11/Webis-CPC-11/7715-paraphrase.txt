The rest of this same floor in the centre building, as well as the whole same floor of the southern building were deemed the composing room. In the wall of the northernmost building, a door had been cut. It was near Mr. Ballantyne's room; once one navigated through this room and maneuvered down three steps, there laid a space intended for the editorial and reportorial staff of the Morning News. Three rooms within this space measured 7x12 feet each. Morgan Bates and I occupied the middle room, with Fields on one side and the dramatic artist on the other. The remainder of the floor was occupied by the city staff. Within the composing room, the telegraph editor claimed a space to himself
